ORGANIZATIONS

NASA

NASA has great plans for the future of space travel! They are currently working towards the completion of the Space Launch System (SLS) and the Orion spacecraft as part of the Artemis program to return to the moon. In addition, they are partnering with private space companies to develop lunar landers and rovers that will assist the arrival of man and woman to the moon in 2024! For more information on NASA activities click here (external link)!

ESA

The European Space Agency (ESA) is NASA's eastern counterpart. They comprise of many European nations and have worked on many satellite missions which gather essential data regarding Earth and the space around it. Upcoming missions include the James Webb space telescope (expected launch: 2021) which will be the successor to the Hubble telescope and the juice satellite (expected launch: 2022) which will be Europe's first satellite mission to Jupiter. For more information on ESA activities click here (external link)!

SpaceX

SpaceX is a traiblazer in the private space company sector. They have worked closely alongside NASA, delivering cargo to the International Space Station (ISS) using their semi-reusable Falcon 9 rockets and Dragon delivery capsule. SpaceX's latest partnership with NASA will enable them to use their technology to send American astronauts to the ISS from American soil on May 27, 2020! (Note: This has not happened since 2011 when the Space Shuttle program was concluded!) For more information on SpaceX activities click here (external link)!
